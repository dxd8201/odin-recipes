Delicious Cajun Crawfish Etouffee straight to your stomach. Poo-Yi!

Home » Crawfish Etouffee Recipe Crawfish Etouffee Recipe by Mike Hultquist · Feb 4, 2022 · 13 Comments Jump to Recipe This Cajun crawfish etouffee recipe is loaded with meaty crawfish tails smothered in a rich gravy with spices and fresh herbs, served over rice. It's huge on flavor and easy to make. One of our very favorites!

Ingredients:

  • Butter
  • All purpose Flower
  • Vegetables
  • Cajun seasonings
  • Cayenne pepper
  • Salt and black bepper
  • Stock
  • Crawfish tails
  • Fresh parsley
  • Cooked rice

Steps:

  1. Make the Roux. Heat a large pan or pot to medium heat and melt the butter. Add the flour and stir to make a roux. Stir constantly for 5 minutes, or until the roux turns a copper color. Do not let the roux burn. A Dutch oven is great for making etouffee.
  2. Cook the Vegetables. Add the onion, bell peppers, celery and garlic. Stir and cook for 5 minutes to soften.
  3. Stock, Seasonings, Simmer. Stir in the Cajun seasonings, cayenne, salt and pepper to taste, and stock or broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 20 minutes, stirring occasionally, to let the flavors develop. I like to simmer 30 minutes.
  4. Crawfish and Parsley. Add the crawfish tails and warm them through. Remove from heat and stir in the parsley.
  5. Serve the Etouffee. Serve over white rice (if desired) and garnish with extra parsley and spicy chili flakes.
